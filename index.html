About
Anastasios
Nyfadopoulos

Artist & Co-founder

In 2015, at the age of 22, Anastasios Nyfadopoulos created "Crisis," the first public sculpture addressing the consequences of the socioeconomic crisis on humanity. Referring to "Crisis" and the impact of the socioeconomic crisis, 17 distinguished professors from various fields shared their insights, opening up the dialogue with the affected communities. Anastasios holds a Master of Fine Arts from the University of Brighton, where he graduated with distinction. He is the winner of the 2018 Global Arts Award by Red Line Art Works, where artists from 27 countries participated. Leading international publications, including The Associated Press and Xinhua, have featured his work. Anastasios is inspired by the fact that we are interconnecting and perpetually changing through every choice we make.

About
Demi
Fotopoulou

Art Manager & Co-founder

Demi Fotopoulou studied with distinction Bachelor in Business Administration at the Athens University of Economics and Business in Greece and with a full scholarship from the Onassis foundation Master of Arts in Art Management and Art Law at the International Hellenic University in Greece. She has worked in the Fine Art field since 2013 and has collaborated with art galleries, municipalities, artists, and collectors. She was the leader of the fundraising and communications team for the creation of the public sculpture “Crisis” by the artist Anastasios Nyfadopoulos and contributed to securing significant sponsors, including Mapei, Bosch, and Lafarge, as well as international press coverage for the sculpture and the artist.
